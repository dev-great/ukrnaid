What Is the Current Situation for Ukraine's Children?

  • 24 March, 2022 | 8:00AM
  • Ukraine

The situation is rapidly worse. Children and families have been harmed by heavy fighting, shelling, and air strikes across Ukraine. Almost every second of the battle, a youngster from Ukraine has become a refugee. There have been at least 3,000 civilian casualties since the international military conflict in Ukraine began on February 24, 2022. These figures are heartbreaking, and they are most certainly an underestimation. Essential services such as schools and hospitals have been harmed as a result of air strikes and explosives. As temperatures drop below freezing, displaced children are forced to spend lengthy days and nights fleeing the fighting in cold, harsh surroundings. We am quite concerned about the hazards of illnesses such as hypothermia, which are particularly dangerous for newborns and small children.

To limit the risk to children's lives and wellbeing, Ukraine Aid is urging all sides to the conflict to agree to an immediate cease of hostilities. After crossing the border from Ukraine, children and families receive assistance from Ukraine's Aid in Romania. This includes assisting them in obtaining an education, offering emotional support, delivering winter and hygiene kits, and providing monetary grants to families in order for them to satisfy basic requirements like as food, rent, and medicines, or to invest in the start-up of new enterprises. overcome the emotional and psychological effects of their conflict and violence experiences, and strengthen their resilience and ability to cope with daily pressures
